Bank Telemarketing Success Prediction

I have worked on this project which aims to develop a predictive model that would determine whether customers of a Portuguese bank would subscribe to a term deposit or not. The data provided encompassed demographic information, credit history, account balance, and contact communication details. The developed binary classification model used this data to minimize marketing costs and increase revenue by targeting customers more likely to take up the term deposit policy. The Gradient Boosting model was implemented to achieve the project's objective and improved the recall score, enabling the bank to target the right customers more effectively while minimizing marketing costs. Additional techniques like K-Fold cross-validation and hyperparameter tuning were used to enhance the model's performance, resulting in an even more optimal model than previous attempts with a higher recall score of 0.923510. The developed model will help the bank make data-driven decisions and design more effective marketing strategies in the future.

Milk Quality Prediction

I have worked on the project aimed to use machine learning algorithms to predict milk quality by considering several factors such as pH, temperature, fat percentage, turbidity, texture, taste, and color. The objective of the project was to help businesses in the food industry identify potential quality issues early on in the manufacturing process and reduce waste, while improving the quality of their products, resulting in cost savings, improved efficiency, and an enhanced customer experience. The project successfully achieved its objective as the machine learning models produced high accuracy levels, with the K-NN classifier being the most effective with an accuracy score of 99.88%. The decision tree and random forest algorithms also performed well, with accuracy scores of 99.76% and 99.81%, respectively. The results indicate the excellent quality of the data and the effectiveness of the models in predicting milk quality, offering businesses a data-driven solution to optimize their manufacturing process and improve their product quality, ultimately contributing to the overall success of the business in the food industry.

Car Rental Management System

The Rent-A-Car Angular with .NET Core and SQL Project is a comprehensive web-based application designed for managing car rental services. The application comprises of a client-side app built on Angular and a server-side application built using .NET Core, with SQL used for storing and managing data. It offers a range of features, including user authentication and authorization using JWT, user roles for admin, employee, and customer, CRUD operations for cars, customers, and rentals, and a dashboard for employees and admin with essential statistics. Customers can browse through cars, view details, and make reservations, with email notifications sent to them when their rental is due soon or overdue. The application is integrated with Google Maps for location and directions. The application is set up by cloning the repository, opening the solution in Visual Studio, building and running the solution to restore NuGet packages, running the SQL script, navigating to the ClientApp directory, installing the necessary dependencies, and running ng serve. Overall, the Rent-A-Car application provides an all-inclusive solution for managing car rental services, ensuring secure, scalable, and efficient management of data and operations.

Latest Blog

Human-centric artificial intelligence focuses on creating intelligent robots that take into account human concerns. This approach has three primary goals: first, to ensure that A.I. understands human concepts in the same way that humans do; second, to enhance human skills with the help of A.I.; and third, to address labor displacement, discrimination, and geopolitical issues related to the development of global A.I. technology.

To create machine intelligence that is more human-like, programmers require the expertise of cognitive scientists, psychologists, and sociologists. This interdisciplinary approach will improve machine cooperation and enable technology to assist surgeons and caregivers in performing creative, intellectual, and emotional tasks. However, ethical A.I. laws must balance ambition with responsibility, including considerations of privacy rights and the potential geopolitical impacts of global A.I. competition.

Solving these issues requires collaboration between universities, governments, and businesses. Universities can promote interdisciplinary research, while companies can invest in smart algorithms and ethical A.I. legislation. Governments should support computer science education, particularly among marginalized groups. The article emphasizes that A.I. is created by humans, modeled after humans, and ultimately affects humans, making human interests an essential factor in its development.
